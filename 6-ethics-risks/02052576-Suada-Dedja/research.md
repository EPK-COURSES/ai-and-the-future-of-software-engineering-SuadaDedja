# Research: Risks, Ethics, and Limitations of AI in Software Engineering



## 1. Introduction to AI Limitations in Software Engineering

Recently, the impact of AI has completely or largely transformed the way we write software. Many tools, including GitHub Copliot and ChatGPT, make it much more
accessible and faster to get code suggestions and automate boring repetitive tasks.However, despite all this, as coding becomes faster, we are also facing many
challenges that we did not initially worry about. Relying blindly on these models introduces technical, legal, and human challenges that we, as future software
engineers, need to take very seriously.



## 2. Technical Limitations and Risks

Another very powerful factor and one of the biggest problems in AI these days is hallucinations. This is when an AI tool, for example, generates a piece of
code that looks completely correct and clean, but in reality this piece of codeis exactly what it is: it has all the errors, is not functional, or refers to libraries that do not even exist. If a developer does not test this code thoroughly, it canbreak the entire system. Studies show that of AI-generated code may contain security vulnerabilities or insecure patterns.For example a Veracode study showed that nearly 45% of AI generated code contains  security vulnerabilities.

A very big problem is also security, since these AI models are trained on massive amounts of public repositories, they often clean up and reuse code
that already has security flaws. This can introduce serious security flaws into a new project without giving any warning to the developer.


## 3. Ethical and Legal Challenges

On the other hand, from an ethical perspective, copyright infringement is a somewhat serious and somewhat ambiguous topic. Furthermore, many AI
tools are trained with code from open-source projects without always respecting their original licenses. This situation is quite dangerous and
moreover creates legal risk, because it is not clear, therefore it is not known and unclear who owns the code and moreover whether a company can be
sued for using the code generated by AI.

Also, data privacy is a big problem. According to surveys, it is shown that 78% of tech professionals believe data privacy is one of the biggest risks of AI.
When programmers copy and paste secret company code into public AI to fix or debug it, they can inadvertently extract private and very sensitive
information. This data can be used by different AI models for training and can sometimes be displayed to other users.


## 4. The Human Factor: Overdependence and Skill Degradation

One of the most important questions is: what happens when programmers no longer understand the code they create? When programmers start to rely too
much on AI, their skills weaken, weaken in such a way that they have a harder time thinking and finding errors. They are unable to think critically and most
importantly, if developers accept every suggestion that comes to them from AI without checking and analyzing it as it should be done, this causes problems
later because it becomes very difficult to fix big problems in the code,since they are unable to understand how it works.

But this is even more problematic for beginners, which means that if students or junior developers use AI to solve every exercise or logical problem, they do
not learn how to become good programmers. They will miss the most important part of learning which is trying, failing and improving, this means that they
will make mistakes, try and learn from difficulties, because this is precisely the key point to building the right and real programming skills.Also a lot of experts warn that relying too much on AI may weaken developers’ debugging and problem-solving skills over time.
 

## 5. Human Supervision and Responsibility

According to studies, AI tools can make work faster and help programmers, but even though this happens and is effective, the problem is precisely that
they should never be used without human control. This happens because Software engineers still bear responsibility for the quality, security and impact
of the systems they create. This means that AI can give ideas and suggestions, but it cannot make decisions on its own without being controlled.
Reports show that many organizations are worried that using AI tools may expose sensitive company data and create privacy risks.
So all that is important is precisely that developers do not trust AI for everything because ultimately if an error or security problem occurs, the
responsibility falls on the human and not on AI. Therefore, AI is seen as an assistant that makes the work easier but that the final control always belongs
to the programmer.

## 6. The Evolving Role of Software Engineers

According to studies and statistics, it is suggested that the role of software engineers in the future will undergo major changes. This is because they will
no longer be just people who write code line by line, but more importantly, they will be people who design systems and also control the way AI is used in the
development of programs. What will happen is that the work will focus less on writing the basic code and more on designing systems, connecting the different parts of a project and checking if everything works properly. This is precisely why developers need to develop strong critical thinking and
analysis skills. They need to be able to check the solutions that AI provides,find errors, and also make sure that the code is safe and meets the needs of
the company. However, although AI will help a lot in programs, humans will continue to have the main role in making decisions and guaranteeing the
quality of systems.

## 7. Conclusion 

AI is becoming more and more important in software engineering, but of course it is still not perfect. There are many problems such as hallucinations,
security problems, privacy concerns and one of the most important facts is that people can lose their skills when they rely too much on AI. This shows
that AI still has many limits and cannot completely replace programmers.

AI should be seen more as a powerful assistant but what is most important it should not be seen as something that does everything by itself. Programmers still have to check the code, understand it and verify whether it is correct or not. In the future, software engineering will not only depend on AI or smarter
tools, but also on developers who know how to think, have their own critical opinion, on programmers who know how to find errors and improve the code
they create. In the end, the one who is responsible is precisely the human, not the AI.
